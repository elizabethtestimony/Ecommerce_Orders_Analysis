# Turning E-Commerce Data into Business Insights

## Project Summary

Businesses generate thousands of customer transactions every day, but raw data alone does not drive better decisions.

In this project, I analyzed an e-commerce orders dataset using Python to identify patterns in customer behavior, product performance, and sales trends. The goal was to transform raw transactional data into meaningful insights that could support business decision-making.

---

## Problem Statement

An online retailer wants to better understand:

- When sales perform best.
- Which customers generate the highest revenue.
- Which product categories drive profit.
- How customer ratings relate to business performance.

This analysis answers these questions through data exploration and visualization.

## Analysis Performed

### Sales Performance
- Monthly sales trends
- Quarterly average sales

### Customer Analysis
- Customer distribution by country
- Customer distribution by age group

### Product Performance
- Most ordered product categories
- Total profit by category
- Average customer ratings

### Profitability Analysis
- Profit generated by each age group
- Relationship between ratings and profit

---

## Insights

After completing the analysis, several patterns emerged:

- Sales varied significantly across months.
- Certain age groups contributed more to total profits.
- Product popularity differed among customer demographics.
- Highly rated products were not always the most profitable.
- A small number of product categories generated a large share of profits.
